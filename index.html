About Me

As mentioned previously, I have been in the Customer and Technical Support industry for 20 years. My first job was Dell technical support under ClientLogic (now Sitel) back in 1998. I was accepted into the position without an A+ certification from CompTIA due to me being able to display enough knowledge and experience with computers to competently perform my duties. I was also the youngest at the time, 17 years old. I started playing with computers early on in my life. My first computer, with help from my father, I built in 1993. That same year, Doom was released and my father purchased a copy for me. That was when I got into game modding, got a copy of The Doom Construction Kit for Christmas 1994, which was a book describing how to mod almost every aspect of the game; from graphics, to level design, to game behaviors. That started my journey down software development and design.

In 2001, I attended High-Tech Institute in Phoenix, Arizona. After six months, the economic downturn and scarcity of jobs kept me from continuing my education there. My professor did urge me at least to get an A+ certification. I spent the last of my money and certified when it was a lifetime certification.

In May of 2009, together with a friend, I founded VonBishop Computer Services and GroundFloor Marketing where we developed websites and utilized SEO and other directory services to bring businesses online with a healthy presence, increasing their revenues and client quantities. On the computer services side, we offered mobile network and computer repair/installation/upgrade services. We also made custom computers as a boutique system integrator.
